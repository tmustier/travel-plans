Perfectly placed

Ca' di Dio sits on the Riva degli Schiavoni waterfront, a 2-minute walk from the San Zaccaria vaporetto stop. San Marco, Dorsoduro, Castello, the Arsenale: everything is on foot from there.

Su e Zo per i Ponti

Sunday 12 April is the beloved "Up and Down the Bridges" walking event. Venetians open hidden courtyards and private gardens across the city. A beautiful thing to stumble into.

Our hotel

Ca' di Dio

Riva degli Schiavoni, Castello Patricia Urquiola VRetreats

A 13th-century pilgrim hospice on the waterfront, reimagined by Patricia Urquiola into one of Venice's most design-forward hotels. 57 rooms. Views straight across to San Giorgio Maggiore.

Urquiola layered Murano glass, Venetian terrazzo, custom fabrics, and bold colour into the historic stone shell. The ground floor opens to the lagoon through tall arched windows. Warm, modern, and unmistakably Venetian.

Not a museum, not a stage set. A place where history and design coexist naturally.

Saturday 11 April

Arrive, explore, taste

Land at 13:30. Water taxi across the lagoon. By 15:00 you're walking through Venice.

Water taxi arrival

Private water taxi from Marco Polo airport straight to the Ca' di Dio dock. 30 minutes across the lagoon. The first view of Venice from the water is unforgettable.

Dorsoduro

Venice's art quarter and most liveable neighbourhood. Start at the Peggy Guggenheim Collection (modern art in a Grand Canal palazzo). Walk to Punta della Dogana for the view.

Bacari crawl

Venice's answer to a wine tour. Stand-up wine bars serving cicchetti (Venetian tapas) and ombre (small glasses of local wine). Hit 3 or 4 between 18:00 and 19:30.

Dinner

Osteria da Fiore (Michelin star, legendary Venetian seafood, family-run since the 1970s) or Oro at Belmond Cipriani on the Giudecca. Book today.

Get lost at night

After 21:00, Venice empties. The alleys, the bridges, the reflections on still water. Follow your feet. This is the best part of the whole trip.

Timing

Peggy Guggenheim closes at 18:00. Bacari are best 17:30-19:30. Dinner reservations from 20:00. The rest is yours.

Sunday 12 April

Morning magic,
deeper Venice

Venice between 7 and 9 in the morning is a different city. Almost empty. Extraordinary light.

San Giorgio Maggiore

7:30 AM. Take the 2-minute vaporetto from San Zaccaria. Climb the bell tower. The single best panoramic view of Venice, with almost nobody there.

Rialto Market

Open Sunday mornings. The fish market is theatrical: lagoon seafood, shouting vendors, morning light through the arches. Coffee at a bar nearby.

Castello

The eastern end of Venice, where locals actually live. The Arsenale shipyard, Via Garibaldi (Venice's widest street), quiet canals, real neighbourhood bars.

Libreria Acqua Alta

A bookshop where books are stored in gondolas, bathtubs, and a full-size boat. Beautifully chaotic. A staircase of books leads to a canal view.

Su e Zo per i Ponti

The walking event runs all day. You'll encounter groups criss-crossing the city, private courtyards opened for the day, hidden gardens. Join a route or just follow the energy.

Where to eat + drink

Bacari, trattorias,
fine dining

Bacari (wine bars)

Venice's soul food. Stand at the bar, order an ombra (small wine) and 2-3 cicchetti. Move on. Repeat.

  • All'Arco
    Near Rialto. The best crostini in Venice. Arrive by 11:30 or 17:30 for the good stuff.
  • Cantina Do Spade
    Open since 1488. Casanova drank here. Try the baccalà mantecato and the sarde in saor.
  • Al Squero
    Tiny bar facing the last working gondola boatyard in Dorsoduro. Aperol spritz with a view.
  • El Sbarlefo
    Quiet Castello spot. Locals only feel. Natural wines and creative cicchetti.

Restaurants

Michelin Osteria da Fiore

San Polo. One star. Family-run since the 1970s. Venetian lagoon seafood at its finest. Book for Saturday dinner.

Michelin Oro Restaurant

Belmond Cipriani, Giudecca. One star. Creative contemporary Italian. They send a private boat to collect you.

At the hotel VERO

Chef Emanuele Scarello (2 stars at Agli Amici). Lagoon ingredients, modern technique. Perfect for Sunday lunch before heading to the airport.

Trattoria alla Madonna

Near Rialto. Old-school Venetian. No frills, incredible seafood, reasonable prices. Great for a casual Sunday lunch.

What to pack

For the day

  • Comfortable walking shoes (cobblestones + bridges)
  • Light layers: 16-17°C, sunny spells
  • Sunglasses
  • Small crossbody bag

For the evening

  • Something nice for dinner (smart-casual)
  • Light jacket for the waterfront

Quick tips

Vaporetto: Buy a 24h ACTV pass (~€25) at the airport or any ACTV booth. Covers all water buses.

Weather: 16-17°C, 11+ hours of daylight. Light rain possible. Bring a packable layer.

Cash: Bacari often prefer cash. Bring €50-100 in small notes.
